Brief story of Typography in India
First printed Word


Arrival of the printing press in india was a result of a happy accident. From a letter written in April 20, 1556 by Father Gaspar Caleza to St. Ignatius of Loyola, it appears that a ship carrying a printing press, with some technicians set sail from Portugal for Abyssinia (present day Ethiopia). At that time those who went to Abyssinia from Portugal rounded the Cape of Good Hope, touched Goa in India, and thence proceeded to Abyssinia. The Patriach designate of Abyssania accompanied the printing press. But the patriach was detained in Goa. He never left Goa nor did the printing press.
European Rule and Early Printing


During the Colonial period, print was mobilised chiefly by Christian missionaries for the purpose of proselytising. These Baptist missionaries published in their own cast type, made their own paper, ink and binding. To overcome the challenges posed by the languages, the missionary staff took the help of locals in translating Indian manuscripts to european languages.
